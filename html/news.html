Dr. Zambrano-Cruzatty awarded prestigious NSF CAREER Award

Dr. Zambrano-Cruzatty was awarded the prestigious NSF CAREER Award. His project will investigate the physics controlling soil motion behavior transitions from solid to liquid-like behaviors. This behavior transition is observed during landslides, where soil behavior can be modeled using solid mechanics principles before triggering and using rheological models during the large deformation process. The study will leverage various computational methods such as Discrete Element Method, constitutive models, and the Material Point Method. The project will also include unique experiments including rheological tests and centrifuge modeling of colum collapse.

Paula Sarmiento completed wind tunnel experiments at NHERI's Boundary Layer Wind Tunnel

Research Assistant and ZGEM member completed experiments at NHERI's Boundary Layer Wind Tunnel. She is studying the interplay between water transport fue to capillary rise and evaporation on eolian sediment transport. She has documented interesting phenomena such as intermitent erosion and modified morphological evolution changing with surface moisture content. This project was funded by The National Science Foundation
